# The Calm Beneath the Chaos

## A Continuous EFT Journey for Conscious Parenting — 20 Rounds

Before you begin, take a slow, gentle breath in, all the way down past the busyness of the day, down into the quiet part of you that is still here underneath everything you carry.

[Pause.]

And let it go, slowly, the way you would set something heavy down at the end of a long shift.

[Pause.]

There is nothing you have to fix in this moment. No one is calling your name. No one needs anything from you right now except the one person who is so often last on the list — you.

[Pause.]

You may be tired in a way that sleep does not touch. You may be carrying a guilt you have not said out loud. That is allowed here. All of it is allowed here.

[Long pause.]

Let your shoulders come down from around your ears. Let your jaw soften. Let your hands rest open, palms loose, the way a child's hands rest when they have finally fallen asleep.

[Pause.]

You do not have to be a better parent in the next twenty minutes. You only have to be honest, and gentle with what is true.

[Pause.]

Your only job in this moment is to breathe, to tap, and to let yourself be met with kindness — perhaps for the first time in a long while.

[Long pause.]

[Begin tapping.]

ROUND 1 — STRETCHED SO THIN

[Side of Hand]

Even though I am stretched so thin I can feel myself fraying at the edges, I choose to admit that I am running on empty…

Even though I have been giving and giving with nothing left to give, I honour how tired I truly am…

[Eyebrow]

So stretched. So thin.

There is so little of me left by the end of the day.

[Side of Eye]

I am pulled in a hundred directions at once.

Everyone needs a piece of me, and the pieces are getting smaller.

[Under Eye]

This tiredness lives in my whole body.

It is in my shoulders, my eyes, the back of my neck.

[Under Nose]

I have forgotten what rested feels like.

I cannot remember the last time I felt full.

[Chin]

I keep going because there is no one else to keep going.

I have not let myself stop, because stopping feels impossible.

[Collarbone]

But right now, in this breath, I am allowed to notice it.

I am allowed to say, out loud, that this is a lot.

[Under Arm]

I am not weak for feeling stretched.

I am a human being who has been carrying more than two hands can hold.

[Top of Head]

I let myself feel exactly how thin I have been stretched.

And I let this be the moment I begin, gently, to come back to myself.

I rest in being good-enough and deeply loving.

And I let that be the ground I stand on from now on.

[Long pause.]

[Take three slow breaths.]

[Pause.]

[Say these words slowly, with meaning:]

I release the belief that I must be a perfect parent to be a good one.

[Pause.]

I release the guilt I have carried for being human on hard days.

[Pause.]

I choose the truth that my presence matters more than my perfection.

[Pause.]

I choose to be the calm, loving, good-enough parent my children need.

[Long pause.]

I am allowed to be tired and still be a wonderful parent.

[Pause.]

I am allowed to make mistakes and to mend them with love.

[Pause.]

I am the calm beneath the chaos, and that calm lives in me.

[Pause.]

My children are lucky to be loved by someone who cares this much.

[Long pause.]

I am still here.

And I am okay.

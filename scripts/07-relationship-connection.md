# Softening Back Toward Each Other

## A Continuous EFT Journey for Relationship Connection — 20 Rounds

Before you begin, take a slow, gentle breath in, and let it out without rushing, the way you might set something heavy down at the end of a long day.

[Pause.]

There is someone you love. And somewhere along the way, a distance crept in between you — quiet, almost unnoticed at first, then somehow harder to cross.

[Pause.]

You don't have to fix any of it right now. You don't have to know what to say, or who was right, or how it all got this way.

[Long pause.]

For these next minutes, all that's asked of you is to feel what's true, and to be kind to yourself while you feel it.

[Pause.]

Let your shoulders come down from where they've been holding. Let your jaw soften. Let your hands rest open.

[Take a slow breath.]

[Pause.]

Notice where in your body the thought of them lives right now — maybe a tightness in the chest, a knot in the belly, a held breath.

[Pause.]

You don't have to change it. Just let it be there, and let yourself be here with it.

[Long pause.]

Your only job in this moment is to keep breathing, and to let the truth be gentle on you.

[Long pause.]

[Pause.]

[Say these words slowly, with meaning:]

I release the belief that protecting myself means staying far away.

[Pause.]

I release the story that the distance between us can never be crossed.

[Pause.]

I choose the truth that love is built in small, brave turnings toward each other.

[Pause.]

I choose to soften, to reach, and to let myself be known.

[Long pause.]

I am someone who can love and be loved with my guard lowered.

[Pause.]

I am allowed to want closeness, and to move toward it.

[Pause.]

The distance is not the end of the story — it is only the part before the turning back.

[Pause.]

I carry warmth toward someone I love, and toward myself.

[Long pause.]

I am still here.

And I am okay.

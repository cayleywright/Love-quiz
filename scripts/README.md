# Continuous EFT — Script Library

A library of fully worded **continuous EFT (tapping) scripts**, written to be read
aloud — by you, or by an AI narrator / text-to-speech voice. Each script is
ready to read top to bottom with no editing, and runs to roughly **5,000 words /
20 rounds** of genuine depth.

Every **instruction and pause is written in square brackets** — `[Pause.]`,
`[Long pause.]`, `[Eyebrow]`, `[Take a breath.]`, `[Begin tapping.]` — so an AI
voice can treat them as stage directions and *do* them, rather than read them
aloud. Everything **outside** the brackets is spoken: the title, the gentle intro
guidance, and the tapping phrases themselves.

These belong to the **Growth** family: gentle, self-contained tools for feeling a
little more settled, a little more present, a little more at home in yourself.

---

## What is continuous EFT?

EFT (Emotional Freedom Techniques), often called **tapping**, combines gentle
fingertip tapping on acupressure points with spoken words that name what you feel
and then gently reframe it. It's "acupressure for the emotions" — no needles, just
your own two fingers.

**Continuous EFT** (the style used here) is the soft, flowing, meditation-like
form. Instead of stopping to rate your distress 0–10 after every round, you simply
keep tapping and keep speaking, letting the words carry you from where you are to
where you'd like to be. It's the gentlest on-ramp for a nervous system that's
already stirred up, and it's ideal for guided audio.

Every script follows the same three-part arc that makes tapping work:

1. **Acknowledge** — tell the truth about what hurts, without minimising it.
2. **Soften / open** — meet it with compassion; loosen the grip.
3. **Reframe / install** — choose a kinder, truer story and let it settle in.

> These scripts are for reflection, self-soothing and general wellbeing. They are
> **not** medical or psychological treatment, and are not a substitute for care
> from a qualified professional. If something heavy surfaces, go gently, and reach
> out for support.

## The tapping points

Tap each point gently 5–7 times (or just rest your fingers there) while the line
is spoken. Use whichever hand you like; either side of the body is fine.

| Order | Point | Where it is |
|------:|-------|-------------|
| 1 | **Side of Hand** ("karate chop") | Fleshy outer edge of the hand, below the little finger |
| 2 | **Eyebrow** | Inner end of the eyebrow, beside the bridge of the nose |
| 3 | **Side of Eye** | On the bone at the outer corner of the eye |
| 4 | **Under Eye** | On the bone directly under the eye |
| 5 | **Under Nose** | In the dip between nose and upper lip |
| 6 | **Chin** | In the crease between lower lip and chin |
| 7 | **Collarbone** | An inch down and an inch out from where the collarbones meet |
| 8 | **Under Arm** | About a hand's width below the armpit, on the side of the body |
| 9 | **Top of Head** | The crown, centre of the top of the head |

The **Side of Hand** is where each round's "set-up" lines are spoken (the *"Even
though… I still…"* lines). The remaining eight points carry the round.

## How to read a script aloud (for a narrator or AI voice)

- **Anything in `[square brackets]` is a stage direction, not speech.** Don't
  read it aloud — *do* it. `[Eyebrow]` means move to the eyebrow point;
  `[Long pause.]` means fall silent and leave space.
- **Slow down.** This is half the technique. Read at roughly the pace of calm
  breathing — slower than normal speech.
- **Honour the pauses.** `[Pause.]` ≈ 2–3 seconds. `[Long pause.]` ≈ 6–10 seconds.
  Don't rush them; the silence is where the settling happens.
- **Warm, even tone.** Kind, unhurried, never clinical. Imagine speaking to
  someone you love who is having a hard day.
- **Let the breath cues breathe.** When a script reaches `[Breathe in slowly.]`,
  leave real space for an actual breath.

> **Tip for setting up an AI voice:** tell it to *silently perform* anything in
> square brackets (pause, change tapping point, take a breath) and *speak* only
> the unbracketed lines, slowly and warmly.

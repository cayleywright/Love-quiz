# Permission to Rest

## A Continuous EFT Journey for Resting & Recovering From Burnout — 20 Rounds

Before you begin, take a slow, gentle breath in, and let the out-breath fall away on its own, without effort.

Pause for a moment, and notice how tired you actually are, without arguing with it.

Breathe out again, even slower, and let your body know that for now there is nothing to push through.

[Long pause.]

Let your shoulders come down from where they have been holding for so long.

Let your jaw loosen, and let your hands open in your lap.

Let the weight you have been carrying rest, just for a little while, on something other than you.

[Pause.]

You do not have to be productive here. You do not have to fix yourself, or perform, or prove anything.

You are allowed to simply be a tired person, here, breathing.

Your only job in this moment is to stop, gently, and let yourself begin to come back to life.

[Long pause.]

[Begin tapping.]

ROUND 1 — I AM BURNED OUT AND EMPTY

[Side of Hand]

Even though I am burned out and running on empty, with nothing left in the tank, I choose to tell the truth about it.

Even though I have been pouring out for so long that there is almost nothing left of me, I accept myself fully, empty and all.

[Eyebrow]

I am burned out.

There, I have said it.

[Side of Eye]

I have been running on fumes for longer than I want to admit.

The tank is empty, and the warning light has been on for a while.

[Under Eye]

I kept going long after the fuel was gone.

I have been driving on memory and willpower.

[Under Nose]

There is a hollowness in the centre of me.

A scraped-out, used-up feeling.

[Chin]

I do not have to pretend I am fine.

Not here, not right now.

[Collarbone]

I am allowed to admit how empty I am.

Naming it does not make it worse, it makes it real.

[Under Arm]

This emptiness is not weakness.

It is the cost of giving for too long with no return.

[Top of Head]

I let myself feel just how depleted I am.

And I meet it with honesty, not shame.
